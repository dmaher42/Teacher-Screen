Out of Africa

One origin, then spread outward

  • Main idea: modern humans emerged in Africa and migrated to other regions.
  • Evidence: older fossils and strong genetic diversity appear in Africa.
  • Pattern: diversity tends to decrease the further people moved from Africa.
Multi-regional

Several regions, connected over time

  • Main idea: modern humans evolved in more than one region, with gene flow between groups.
  • Evidence: it tries to explain regional continuity in different parts of the world.
  • Question: how much local evolution happened before modern humans became dominant?

Big idea: the map shows movement, but the evidence decides the story.

If students can explain where the evidence comes from, they can explain why one theory is stronger than the other.
2. Settlements become civilisations

Why did the earliest societies develop near rivers?

Water made farming possible, farming fed people, and stable food supplies let towns grow into civilisations.

Nile
Egypt grew around the Nile because water, fertile soil, and transport all came together.
Tigris / Euphrates
Babylon and Mesopotamia developed where river valleys supported farming and trade.
Indus
The Indus Valley used river water and irrigation to support large, organised settlements.
Yellow River
Ancient China also grew beside a river system that supported agriculture and government.
Water creates stability Rivers + farming + trade = permanent settlement
